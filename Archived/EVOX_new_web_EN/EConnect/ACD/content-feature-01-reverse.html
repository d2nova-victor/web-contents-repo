An IVR can help improve caller experience in a number of ways

The first thing about IVR is that an incoming calls get answered immediately, so callers are not kept online listening to the ringing tone waiting for the call to be answered.

For calls that are routine, such as questions about office hours or account balance etc., pre-recorded messages or text to voice systems can provide callers with a quick answer without needing a live agent.

And it saves time for the agents, so they can attend to callers who need more personalized support.

For many services not all agents are familiar with the issues or products the caller is interested in. For example, for a banking business, some agents are experts in loans, and others are expert in credit card service. And IVR can find out the caller’s interest to direct the call to the right agent.

In our IVR we also allow the caller is specifically reach the same agent to follow up on any unfinished transaction.

Instead of just putting the caller on hold, a well designed IVR can be collecting information like account number etc. so that the agent can more quickly assist the callers when they are connected.

However, if this information is not going to be used by the agent, then it will actually make the experience worse for the caller.

The most common complaint by customers is that they have to repeat the same information again when calling a support center!!!
