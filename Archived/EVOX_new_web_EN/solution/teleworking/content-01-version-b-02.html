Telecommuting is a WIN WIN for employers and employees

Higher productivity, happier employees

Telecommuting gives employees control over their work hours and lifestyle. Employees who are not bound to fixed work hours and an office location can avoid unnecessary stress from rush hour commutes. Corporations that have implemented telecommuting have benefitted from dramatic improvements in productivity and morale, leading to better business results.

80% of employees prefer to telework. Is your company ready?

Access to a larger talent pool

With telecommuting, employers can access talent pools outside of their geographical location to find the right talent. Even if they are physically remote, they can easily reach and collaborate with their colleagues with cloud communication services like EVOX. Telecommuting also expands the workforce, allowing parents caring for young children or workers caring for aging parents and handicapped workers to join the workforce.

Cost savings

Businesses that implement telecommuting have also found significant cost savings, not just in the office space rental and utility costs, but also in capital expenses.

Avoid work disruptions

Another benefit of telecommuting is to reduce and even avoid any work disruption due to unexpected events such as power failure, traffic stoppage, or natural disasters like earthquake, typhoon, or contagious diseases. With total mobility, employees can continue to work from home or any remote location when the office is shut down.
